Skills and Experience

As I mentioned before, I’m currently on a path leading towards sales, at the moment more specifically ticket sales, in collegiate and professional sports. I’ve received ticket sales experience in Division 1 Athletics working for the UC Ticket Office running the Fan First Center where we generated $3300 in ticket sales last season up from $1000 they did the previous year. The professional sports ticket sales experience I have so far is I completed this summer a ticket sales/community relations internship with the Akron Racers, one of six women’s national fast pitch softballs teams in the country.

A lot of people even in Akron, down the road of the stadium no less, never heard of the Akron Racers so it is a real challenge trying to sell a lesser known or unknown product when cold calling businesses and non-profit organizations or going out to places in the area in person. It's definitely not the same as at UC when obviously everyone knows our athletics and especially easier to sell tickets to current fans in the arena when I ran the Fan First Center during our basketball season. What was nice about just us three interns doing the selling is we got to approach our two supervisors with new ideas and tweak things and roll with it. This internship would have been very daunting if we hadn't received professional sales consulting from Kathy Burrows, the owner of Sold Out Seating who has worked with teams in the NBA, WNBA, MLB, NHL so it was a tremendous opportunity to learn all summer. She came in every other week to help us with our sales plans and help us execute better to work towards her ambitious goal of doubling attendance from last year.

I'm glad I picked a small team to work for regardless as I got as much hands on sales experience possible in my opinion. I was told by my supervisor that the Racers increased ticket sales by 10-15% from last season so I'm very happy I was able to help them achieve that. The experiences I have had in the short amount of time have at times pushed me out of my comfort zone, which have allowed me to mature as a confident and competent professional. Upon graduation, I hope to find a firm that has a culture of developing young salesmen that has a high rate of landing full time sales careers in professional sports.

Professional Sports Teams’ Marketing Efforts in Columbus, Ohio (July 2015 - June 2016)
View my research here.

The purpose of this study was to chronicle the efforts made by several professional sports franchises to gain a share of the Columbus, Ohio market. Specifically, we examined if and how these teams, which generally share a cooperative relationship, compete for a share of this neutral market. This study served both as a history of the Columbus sports market, and as a granular look at how professional sports franchises balance their relationship as competitors and collaborators. This information will help future scholars and practitioners in sport, marketing, management, and other fields by shedding light on each side’s strategy, and potential areas for mutual benefit.

I was fortunate enough to interview front office executives of the Cleveland Browns, Cincinnati Bengals, Indianapolis Colts and the Columbus Sports Commission. The research was awarded a $1700 research grant from the Undergraduate Research Committee in Human Services which gave me the opportunity to present it at the 2016 Sports Entertainment and Venues Tomorrow Conference at the University of South Carolina as a poster presentation.
